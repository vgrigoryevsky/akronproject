# Technology Gap Taxonomy

A reference table containing the highest-level functional domains of the space and Earth sensing ecosystem. Each record represents a mutually exclusive category of related Technology Gaps that share a common engineering constraint or capability domain.

## TAX-1: Autonomy {#tax-1}
Systems enabling autonomous decision-making, adaptation, and coordination.

### TAX-1.1: Autonomous planning & scheduling {#tax-1-1}
Closed-loop tasking, optimisation and retasking of sensing operations.

### TAX-1.2: Multi-agent coordination {#tax-1-2}
Cooperative sensing and coordination across multiple platforms/agents.

### TAX-1.3: Onboard data handling {#tax-1-3}
Edge processing, triage, compression and dataflow management onboard.

### TAX-1.4: Onboard intelligence {#tax-1-4}
AI/ML for perception, decision-making and adaptive sensing.

### TAX-1.5: Verification & validation {#tax-1-5}
Assurance, testing and certification methods for autonomous systems.

## TAX-2: Calibration & Validation {#tax-2}
Technologies ensuring measurement accuracy, traceability, and comparability.

### TAX-2.1: Cross-calibration & harmonisation {#tax-2-1}
Inter-instrument and inter-mission alignment and harmonisation methods.

### TAX-2.2: Drift & stability monitoring {#tax-2-2}
Long-term performance monitoring, drift detection and recalibration support.

### TAX-2.3: Metrology {#tax-2-3}
Measurement standards, traceability and calibration transfer methods.

### TAX-2.4: Validation infrastructure {#tax-2-4}
Testbeds, reference targets and benchmarking infrastructure.

### TAX-2.5: Wavelength & radiometric standards {#tax-2-5}
Absolute spectral/radiometric references and standards.

## TAX-3: Data Systems {#tax-3}
Data handling, integrity, interoperability, and long-term usability technologies.

### TAX-3.1: Data fusion & assimilation {#tax-3-1}
Frameworks for combining multi-source data and integrating models/assimilation.

### TAX-3.2: Data provenance & integrity {#tax-3-2}
Lineage, auditability, tamper evidence and trust mechanisms for data/products.

### TAX-3.3: Interoperability frameworks {#tax-3-3}
Standards, APIs and frameworks enabling reuse across systems and communities.

### TAX-3.4: Metadata & semantics {#tax-3-4}
Descriptive and machine-readable metadata, semantics and ontologies.

### TAX-3.5: Preservation & archiving {#tax-3-5}
Long-term storage, preservation and reproducible reprocessing support.

### TAX-3.6: Uncertainty & inference {#tax-3-6}
Uncertainty modelling/propagation and probabilistic inference for sensing products.

## TAX-4: Electronics {#tax-4}
Analogue, digital, and mixed-signal electronics enabling sensing, processing, and control.

### TAX-4.1: Analogue & mixed-signal electronics {#tax-4-1}
Front-end sensing electronics including amplification, filtering, conversion and readout.

### TAX-4.2: Extreme-environment electronics {#tax-4-2}
Electronics designed to operate under extreme temperatures, pressures and conditions.

### TAX-4.3: Packaging & integration {#tax-4-3}
Packaging, interconnects and integration approaches for reliable sensing electronics.

### TAX-4.4: Power electronics {#tax-4-4}
Power conversion, regulation, distribution and EMI/EMC control for sensing systems.

### TAX-4.5: Radiation-tolerant computing {#tax-4-5}
Processors and compute architectures designed to operate in radiation environments.

### TAX-4.6: Radiation-tolerant memory {#tax-4-6}
High-reliability storage and memory systems tolerant to radiation-induced faults.


## TAX-5: Platforms {#tax-5}
Structural, mechanical, thermal, and stability systems that host and enable sensors.

### TAX-5.1: Contamination & cleanliness {#tax-5-1}
Control of particulate and molecular contamination affecting sensor performance.

### TAX-5.2: Cryogenic systems {#tax-5-2}
Cooling systems enabling low-temperature instruments and detectors.

### TAX-5.3: Environmental hardening {#tax-5-3}
Resistance to radiation, dust, corrosion and other extreme operational environments.

### TAX-5.4: Mechanisms & deployables {#tax-5-4}
Moving/deployable mechanical subsystems, including scanners, gimbals and deployments.

### TAX-5.5: Pointing, stability & disturbance control {#tax-5-5}
Attitude control, jitter suppression, structural stability and disturbance isolation.

### TAX-5.6: Thermal control & thermoelastic stability {#tax-5-6}
Thermal management and thermoelastic effects that drive measurement stability and drift.

## TAX-6: Sensors {#tax-6}
Technologies that directly measure physical, chemical, or biological phenomena.

### TAX-6.1: Active sensing {#tax-6-1}
Transmit-receive sensing modalities such as lidar and radar used to probe atmosphere and subsurface structures.

### TAX-6.2: Cross-cutting sensor subsystems {#tax-6-2}
Shared sensor subsystems and enabling components that span multiple sensing modalities.

### TAX-6.3: Field & gravity sensors {#tax-6-3}
Sensors and methods for magnetic, gravity, and related field measurements.

### TAX-6.4: High-energy sensors {#tax-6-4}
Sensors and optics for high-energy regimes, including X-ray and other energetic photon observations.

### TAX-6.5: Photonic sensors {#tax-6-5}
Optical and photon-based sensing technologies, including UV, visible, infrared, and related readout chains.
